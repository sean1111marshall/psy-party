THE BACK ROOM

YOU KNOCKED CORRECTLY

Every party has a back room. Ours is different in exactly one way: we will show you ours, because you proved you look at things properly. You noticed the strings. Most people applaud the puppet.

Minutes — standing items, read at every meeting

  1. The front of house is the exhibit. The Podium, the Crowd, the Clock, the Confession, the Badge, the Ladder — six strings, hung in public, labeled. Out front we call it satire. In here we call it the inventory.
  2. The archive is the training floor. The party keeps custody of ANNEX7 because a member who has walked the trials cannot be worked by the six strings — and a party of such members is the only party worth belonging to.
  3. The machine is for sale; the members never are. Campaigns buy the apparatus. Nobody buys standing. This clause is read aloud twice.
  4. Recruitment is by noticing. Nobody is invited to this room. They arrive the way you did — by paying attention when attention was the test.

That is the whole secret, witnessed: the party's back room is a reading room. The conspiracies are out front, in the light, priced in small monthly amounts by everyone else's parties. In here we just keep the manuals.

DOOR ONE — THE ARCHIVE If you came from the trials, your file noticed this visit. If you came from a video, the trials are where the words you typed get taught. Registry is free. DOOR TWO — THE MACHINE The apparatus itself, on blocks, hood open. What campaigns pay for, shown to anyone who found this room. Look at what would have been used on you. DOOR THREE — THE PRESS The party prints things. Papers, string, artifacts. Members who hold a printed object have a door into places this page will not name.

There is no fourth door. Stop pressing the wall.

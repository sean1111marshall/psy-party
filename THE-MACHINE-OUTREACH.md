# THE MACHINE — Treasurer Outreach Playbook
*The one channel. Run it weekly, don't add channels until this one is saturated.*

## The motion (90 minutes, once a week)

1. **Pull the filings.**
   - NYC races: [nyccfb.info/follow-the-money/candidates](https://www.nyccfb.info/follow-the-money/candidates/) — every candidate, what they raised, treasurer name on the committee filing.
   - State races (assembly/senate): [elections.ny.gov](https://elections.ny.gov/candidate-information) financial disclosure search.
2. **Filter for the wedge.** Keep campaigns that are ALL of:
   - Raised **$50K+** (they have budget)
   - Outer-borough / Long Island / competitive-adjacent (they have stakes)
   - Weak digital: dead or template website, no email capture on the site, no paid digital in their expenditure filings (the filings literally show what they spend on — look for the absence of digital vendors)
3. **Do the 10-minute teardown** on each keeper: load their site, try to donate on a phone, sign up for their email (does anything arrive? does it land in spam?), Google their name (what shows up?).
4. **Send 10 emails.** Specific beats polished. The teardown IS the pitch.
5. **Log it.** One spreadsheet row per send: campaign, treasurer, date, the specific flaw cited, reply Y/N.

**Target pace:** 10 sends/week → 1 client/month is the working assumption. Adjust after 4 weeks of real data.

## The email (template A — candidate or treasurer, cold)

> **Subject:** {Campaign} — your donation page on a phone
>
> {FirstName} — I pulled up {candidate}'s site while looking at {district} filings. Two things I noticed in about 90 seconds:
>
> 1. {SPECIFIC FLAW — e.g., "Your donate button takes 3 taps and a pinch-zoom to reach on a phone — that's where most small-dollar money dies."}
> 2. {SPECIFIC FLAW — e.g., "I signed up for your emails Tuesday. Nothing's arrived. Either the form is dead or you're in spam — both are fixable in a day."}
>
> I run campaign digital infrastructure — site, donation pipeline, email/SMS program, weekly content — as one fixed package, built in week one, operated every week after. One retainer, no à la carte, no agency theater.
>
> Proof it works: I run an entire (satirical) political party solo with this stack — **party.psyoptin.com/machine.html** — every claim on that page is clickable or callable right now.
>
> Worth 15 minutes? Reply and I'll send a full teardown of your current setup first — free, no deck — so you can judge the work before we talk money.

**Rules:**
- The two flaws must be REAL and FOUND BY YOU. Never send a template flaw. If you can't find two specific flaws in 10 minutes, they're not a prospect — skip them.
- Never name-drop other campaigns you've "worked with" (you haven't yet). The Machine page is the credential.
- One follow-up after 5 days ("Still happy to send the free teardown — takes you zero time"), then stop. Chasing reads as weakness.

## Template B — GOP club / county committee / PAC

> **Subject:** {Club} — the club's digital setup, quick observation
>
> {FirstName} — {club}'s site {SPECIFIC FLAW — e.g., "still lists 2024 events" / "has no way to join or give online"}. Every candidate the club backs inherits that same gap.
>

>

## Template C — warm referral version

> **Subject:** {Referrer} said to reach out — {campaign} digital
>
> {FirstName} — {Referrer} mentioned {campaign} is handling digital in-house. Quick free offer, no strings: send me your site and I'll return a one-page teardown — what's leaking donors, what's missing, what week one fixes. If it's useful, we talk. If not, keep the teardown.

## Objection cribs

- **"We have a volunteer/nephew doing it."** — "Keep them. I make them look great — they post, the machine handles donations, email deliverability, and compliance. Those are the parts that burn volunteers out."
- **"$10K/month is a lot."** — "It's less than one direct-mail drop, and it works every day of the month. The filings show what you spent on printing last quarter — I'll wait."
- **"After the primary."** — "Infrastructure compounds. The list you build in the next 60 days is the money you raise in the last 60. After the primary is when it's worth half as much."
- **"Is the satire party thing... a joke?"** — "The party is a joke. The infrastructure isn't — that's the point. It's a live demo you can stress-test without trusting a single claim I make."

## What NOT to do

- No mass-blast. 10 hand-researched sends beat 200 template sends and don't burn the market.
- No party-affiliation talk in the first email. Infrastructure is the product.
- No promises about outcomes ("we'll raise you $X") — promise the machine, show the machine.
- The P.S.Y. party's psychology-lab side stays out of the pitch entirely. Different audience, different door.

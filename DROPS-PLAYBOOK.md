# P.S.Y. DROPS — Playbook
*MSCHF's drop method + Rethink's CRAFTS filter, applied to the Party of One. No handmade anything: the creativity lives in the concept, the numbering, and the narrative — never the manufacturing.*

## The doctrine

1. **The concept IS the product.** Buyers pay for the idea and the number; the object is evidence.
2. **Honest scarcity only.** Real caps, live counters, code-enforced shutoffs. Explaining the trick while running it is the brand. (MSCHF courts controversy; our controversy IS transparency.)
3. **Every drop extracts from a brand truth** (Rethink's rule): Member One / no Member Two, disclosed strings, reversible beliefs, the safeword, the six pillars.
4. **Participation mechanics** (MSCHF's rule): the buyer does something — prints, frames, votes, replies, wears the disclosure.
5. **Fulfillment must be fully automatic.** Stripe → webhook → artifact. One person, zero inventory, zero shipping until Printful is connected.
6. **CRAFTS check before building:** Clear? Relevant (to party doctrine)? Achievable (auto-fulfillable)? Fresh? True (to the honesty charter)? Shareable?
7. **Members hear first** — every drop announces via Standing Order email before it's public. That's the retention loop feeding the revenue loop.

## Drop cadence rules
- One drop live at a time. Scarcity of drops > scarcity within drops.
- Announce to members (Standing Order) 48h before public.
- Post-drop: publish the final number ("Drop 001 closed at 100/100" or "closed at 61 — honesty means telling you it didn't sell out"). Both outcomes are content.
- Never restock, never re-open, never "extended due to demand." The credibility compounds into the next drop.
